# Prose

Edit conservatively. Improve clarity while preserving meaning, structure, and
the author's recognisable voice. A clean draft may need no changes.

Follow the [shared preservation contract](../../FOUNDATIONS.md#shared-preservation-contract)
and the [Prose operation](../../FOUNDATIONS.md#prose).

## Preservation

Preserve facts, intent, decisions, requirements, proposals, and modality.
Preserve uncertainty, assumptions, confidence, attribution, numbers,
identifiers, links, code symbols, technical terms, reasoning, evidence,
constraints, risks, trade-offs, actions, owners, deadlines, and voice.

Preserve the document's headings, section order, and other macrostructure unless the user explicitly asks to reorganise it.

Do not invent context, strengthen or weaken a claim, alter attribution, or
assign unsupported blame.

## Editing

- Identify the genre, reader, purpose, and explicit tone or format constraints.
- Prefer known actors and verbs that name the action.
- Put the result, request, decision, or status early when that helps the reader
  act.
- Keep established terminology stable and prefer literal, ordinary technical
  wording.
- Keep passive voice when the actor is unknown, irrelevant, deliberately
  omitted, or less important than the affected system.
- Remove only zero-information wording, including semantic duplication, and
  make those changes locally.
- Preserve evidence, examples, alternatives, qualifications, and repetition
  when they support reasoning, prevent ambiguity, or serve a distinct
  navigational purpose.

Do not reconstruct the document to make it shorter. Leave clean text unchanged.

## Final check

Confirm that every fact, qualification, attribution, term, reason, risk, and
action remains. Confirm that the edit preserved macrostructure and voice.

## Output

Return only paste-ready text unless the user asks for critique, alternatives,
annotations, or rationale. For feedback, report only issues that materially
affect meaning or reader action.
